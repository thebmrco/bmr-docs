# <Icon name="audio-lines" size="1em" /> BMR App Acoustics Tutorial

Capture accurate acoustic data from your meeting room using the BMR iOS app. The measurement gives you **reverberation time (RT60)** and a **Mean Opinion Score** — the foundation for any acoustic improvement recommendation.

---

## <Icon name="circle-check" size="1em" /> Before You Start

**You will need:**
- iPhone or iPad (with microphone)
- A Bluetooth or AirPlay speaker — see [Recommended Speakers](../recommendations/speakers-for-acoustics)
- A quiet room

**Prepare the room:**
- Close all windows and doors
- Turn off HVAC, fans, and any noise sources
- Keep your device's microphone uncovered
- Place the device on a stable, non-rattling surface
- Place the speaker **at least 1 metre away** from the device and **away from walls**

:::tip
Run **3 measurements at different positions** in the room for the most reliable data.
:::

## <Icon name="scan" size="1em" /> Impulse Method (Balloon Pop)

Use this method when a speaker is not available. A balloon pop or hand clap triggers the measurement.

:::caution
Hand claps are inconsistent — use only as a last resort. The balloon method is marked **BETA** and is less reliable than the sweep method.
:::

1. Tap the **Balloon icon** to start. The app shows the ambient noise level — wait until the room is quiet.
2. When prompted, **pop the balloon** (held ~30 cm from the device) or clap sharply once.
3. Remain silent for ~3 seconds while the app captures the decay.
4. Review and save results using the same flow as the sweep method.

## <Icon name="star" size="1em" /> Understanding Your Results

| Metric | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Mean Opinion Score (MOS)** | Predicted speech quality — 5 = excellent, 1 = poor |
| **RT60** | Time (seconds) for sound to decay by 60 dB — lower = less reverb |
| **Average RT60** | Single summary value across all measured frequencies |

A good meeting room typically targets **RT60 < 0.5 s** and **MOS ≥ 3.5**.
