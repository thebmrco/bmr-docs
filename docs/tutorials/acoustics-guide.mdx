# <Icon name="audio-lines" size="1em" /> BMR App Acoustics Tutorial

Capture accurate acoustic data from your meeting room using the BMR iOS app. The measurement gives you **reverberation time (RT60)** and a **Mean Opinion Score (MOS)** — the foundation for any acoustic improvement recommendation.

The app guides you through a **5-step flow**, taking measurements at three positions in the room for a reliable, representative result.

---

## <Icon name="circle-check" size="1em" /> Before You Start

**You will need:**
- iPhone or iPad with the BMR app
- A Bluetooth or AirPlay speaker — see [Recommended Speakers](../recommendations/speakers-for-acoustics)
- A quiet room

**Prepare the room:**
- Close all windows and doors
- Turn off HVAC, fans, and any noise sources
- Keep the device's microphone uncovered
- Place the speaker **at least 1 m / 3 ft away from the wall**

The results screen shows:

| Result | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Mean Opinion Score (MOS)** | Predicted speech quality — 5 = excellent, 1 = poor |
| **Average RT60** | Average reverberation time across all positions taken |
| **RT60 graph** | T20 reverberation time across frequency bands |

A good meeting room typically targets **RT60 < 0.5 s** and **MOS ≥ 3.5**.

## <Icon name="scan" size="1em" /> Impulse Method (Balloon Pop)

Use this method when a speaker is not available. A balloon pop or hand clap triggers the measurement.

:::caution
Hand claps are inconsistent — use only as a last resort. The balloon method is less reliable than the sweep method.
:::

1. Tap the **Balloon icon** to start. Wait until the ambient noise level is low.
2. **Pop the balloon** (held ~30 cm from the device) or clap sharply once when prompted.
3. Remain silent for ~3 seconds while the app captures the decay.
4. Review and save results.
