# Digital Twin

Transform your physical meeting rooms into detailed 3D digital models that serve as a single source of truth for your entire organization.

## What is a Digital Twin?

A digital twin is a precise virtual representation of your meeting room that includes:

- **3D room geometry** - Exact dimensions, walls, windows, and architectural features
- **Furniture placement** - Accurate positioning of tables, chairs, and fixtures
- **Technology integration** - Cameras, displays, microphones, and speakers with coverage zones
- **Environmental data** - Acoustics, lighting, and sightlines

## Why Create a Digital Twin?

### Single Source of Truth
Avoid information silos by maintaining one accurate model that everyone in your organization can reference. No more conflicting room specs or outdated floor plans.

### Better Planning
Test furniture layouts, technology placements, and room configurations virtually before making any physical changes. Identify potential issues early and optimize your space for maximum collaboration.

### Accurate Documentation
Maintain precise records of your meeting room assets, configurations, and specifications. Essential for facilities management, IT support, and ongoing maintenance.

### Cost Savings
Reduce expensive mistakes by visualizing changes before implementation. Make informed decisions about technology investments and room upgrades.

## Creating Your Digital Twin

### 1. Room Scanning
Use 3D scanning technology to capture:
- Room dimensions and geometry
- Architectural features (doors, windows, columns)
- Existing furniture and fixtures
- Current technology placement

### 2. Data Processing
The scan data is processed to create:
- Clean 3D geometry
- Accurate measurements
- Photo-realistic textures
- Navigable 3D model

### 3. Enhancement
Add critical information:
- Technology specifications
- Coverage zones (audio, video)
- Acoustic properties
- Equipment metadata

### 4. Validation
Verify the digital twin against:
- Physical measurements
- Existing documentation
- Technology specifications
- User requirements

## Using Your Digital Twin

### Design & Planning
- Test different furniture layouts
- Optimize camera and display placement
- Visualize technology coverage zones
- Plan for future upgrades

### Documentation
- Generate accurate floor plans
- Create equipment inventories
- Document cable routing
- Maintain as-built records

### Analysis
- Evaluate sight lines and viewing angles
- Assess acoustic performance
- Identify blind spots in camera coverage
- Optimize microphone placement

### Collaboration
- Share 3D models with stakeholders
- Present design options to decision makers
- Coordinate with IT and facilities teams
- Get remote approval on changes

## Maintaining Your Digital Twin

Your digital twin should evolve with your physical space:

- **Update after changes** - Modify the digital twin when you make physical changes
- **Regular audits** - Periodically verify the model matches reality
- **Version control** - Track changes over time
- **Documentation** - Record all modifications and upgrades

## Common Questions

**How accurate is a digital twin?**
Typically within 1-2cm for room dimensions when using professional scanning equipment.

**How often should we update it?**
Update whenever you make physical changes to the room. Audit annually even without changes.

**Can we create twins of existing rooms?**
Yes! Digital twins work for both new designs and existing spaces.

**What equipment do we need?**
Professional 3D scanning can be done with specialized equipment or even modern smartphones with LiDAR sensors.

**How long does it take?**
Scanning a typical meeting room takes 15-30 minutes. Processing and validation add 1-2 hours.
