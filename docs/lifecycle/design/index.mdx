# <Icon name="design" size="1em" /> Design Phase

The **Design Phase** turns your Room Data into a complete, validated room setup — a vision of what the room should be. All design work happens in **BMR Studio**, the unified 3D workspace where rooms are modeled, refined, and managed across the full lifecycle.

---

## <Icon name="palette" size="1em" /> BMR Studio

:::info Beta feature — available to all users
BMR Studio is currently in **Beta**, but enabled for everyone. A full public release is coming soon.
:::

**BMR Studio** is the single 3D environment used for all room design work in BMR. There is one version of BMR Studio — it is where you model rooms, place equipment, manage design versions, and eventually mark a design as Deployed.

### What you can do in BMR Studio

- **Place and configure equipment** from the catalog — cameras, screens, speakers, codecs, furniture, and more
- **Model room geometry** and document infrastructure, cable routing, and layout
- **Review coverage overlays** — camera field of view, screen visibility, and audio coverage
- **Create and compare multiple design versions** for the same room
- **Generate an equipment list** automatically from placed items
- **Move a design through approval** and mark it as Deployed when installed

### How to open BMR Studio

1. Open the room on the **web platform**
2. Navigate to **Design**
3. Click **Open Studio** — the 3D editor loads with your room scan as the base

:::tip
The richer your Discovery data — scan quality, marker coverage, acoustic measurements — the more accurately BMR Studio reflects the real room.
:::

---

## <Icon name="copy" size="1em" /> Design Versions

Each room can have **multiple design versions** in BMR Studio. This lets you:

- Explore different equipment configurations or layout options side by side
- Preserve historical versions as the room evolves
- Track which version was actually deployed

One design version can be designated as the **Deployed Design** — the authoritative record of the room as it currently exists. All other versions represent proposed or historical states.

---

## <Icon name="3d-ar-design" size="1em" /> AR Design

The BMR App includes an **AR Design mode** that lets you place 3D elements directly into the physical room in real scale. While standing inside the space, you can:

- Position displays, cameras, microphones, and furniture
- Test placement scenarios at actual scale
- Evaluate sightlines and spatial constraints
- Experiment with configurations before committing to a design in BMR Studio

AR Design bridges the gap between concept and physical space — useful for quick exploration before detailed studio work.

## <Icon name="help-circle" size="1em" /> Request Design Assistance

If you prefer to have BMR handle the design, you can request assistance through our website. A BMR designer will use your Room Data to produce a complete design package, which is then uploaded to the room for your review.

**What you receive:**
- Full design with placed equipment
- Equipment list with key components
- Acoustic treatment recommendation
- Coverage visuals (camera, audio, screen) where applicable

To request assistance, visit **[bettermeetingrooms.com](https://www.bettermeetingrooms.com)** and use the **Request Assistance** option.

---

## <Icon name="circle-check" size="1em" /> Reviewing & Approving a Design

Whether created in BMR Studio or delivered by BMR, the review process is the same:

1. Open the room's **Design** section to view available versions
2. Inspect the 3D layout, coverage visuals, and equipment list
3. Add comments or request adjustments
4. **Approve** the design when ready
5. Once physically installed, **mark it as Deployed** — it becomes the Deployed Design and the room's live record

---

## <Icon name="circle-check" size="1em" /> Outputs of the Design Phase

- An **approved design** with a complete equipment list
- **Coverage visuals** — camera view, screen visibility, audio coverage
- **Acoustic recommendation** based on measurements and use case
- A **full version history** tied to the room
