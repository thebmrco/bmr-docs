# <Icon name="compass" size="1em" /> Compass

The **Compass** captures the room's real-world orientation during scanning, so that walls, equipment, and design elements can be discussed using cardinal directions — north, south, east, west — instead of vague references like "the wall over there" or "the one with the door".

It's a small piece of metadata, but it removes a lot of friction once more than one person is involved in a design.

**Why it matters**

- Reference walls and equipment using real directions — "the screen is on the **west wall**" is unambiguous to everyone in the conversation, regardless of who is in the room.
- Hybrid design reviews work better — designers on web, technicians on-site, and IT remote can all align on the same orientation without needing photos or pointing.
- Foundation for spatially aware features — natural daylight modelling, AV equipment positioning relative to windows, and other orientation-dependent checks coming next.

## <Icon name="scan" size="1em" /> How it's captured

The compass orientation is captured **automatically during the room scan** — no separate step is required.

- The BMR App uses the iPhone or iPad's built-in magnetometer to determine which direction the device is facing while you walk through the room.
- The orientation is stored alongside the scan geometry as part of the room's **Room Data**.
- It syncs to the back-end so the web platform and BMR Studio can use it during the Design phase.

:::tip Get an accurate reading
- Hold the device level during scanning. A device tilted heavily up or down gives the magnetometer less to work with.
- Avoid strong metal masses (large filing cabinets, server racks, structural steel) close to the device while the scan begins — they can deflect the compass.
- If the captured orientation looks wrong in BMR Studio, re-scan the room from outside any nearby metal influence and the orientation will update.
:::

## <Icon name="palette" size="1em" /> Where you see it later

Compass orientation surfaces wherever the room is shown in 3D:

- **BMR Studio (Web)** — the room appears in its true geographic orientation in the 3D view. A compass widget shows which direction is north so you can always relate the model back to the physical room.
- **BMR Studio in the BMR App (iPad / iPhone)** — same orientation and compass widget, useful when reviewing a design on-site.
- **Room Overview** — the captured orientation is part of the room's Room Data and is visible whenever the room is displayed.

This means that anyone opening the room — designer, IT, facilities, or a remote stakeholder — sees the same orientation. Calling out "the camera goes on the **north-east** corner" is then concrete and shared, instead of relying on whoever happened to take the photos.

## <Icon name="circle-check" size="1em" /> What's next

Compass orientation is the first of several spatially aware features. Upcoming uses include:

- Daylight and glare considerations driven by the room's orientation relative to the sun.
- Default Room Configuration recommendations that take orientation into account — for example, suggesting screen placement away from window-facing walls.
- Equipment placement guidance for cameras and microphones that depends on where windows and doors actually are.
